Frequently Asked Questions

What are the most important things I should know about riding a bike?

Safety First! Always obey the rules of the road. Obey all traffic signals, signs, and laws. Get in the mindset of “driving” your bike—not just “riding” your bike. This will help you be a more focused and legally compliant bike rider.

How can I tell if my helmet is old and I need a new one?

Check for cracks or visible damage. Ensure the helmet still fits snugly. Replace it if it's over 5 years old or if it’s been in a crash.

My bike has been in storage is it safe to ride?

Check tire pressure and inspect for cracks. Test the brakes to ensure they work properly. Verify the chain is rust-free and lubricated. Ensure handlebars and seat are secure before riding.

What rules should I follow when riding my bike?

Wear a helmet and follow traffic laws. Stay visible and alert, using lights and hand signals. Ride predictably, using bike lanes when available. Be cautious at intersections and respect pedestrians.
